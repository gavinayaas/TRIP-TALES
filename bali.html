Bali, Indonesia

Temples at sunrise, rice terraces in the afternoon, and coastal sunsets that make you quiet down inside.

Ubud Uluwatu Canggu

Highlights to experience

Uluwatu cliffs & sunset

Dramatic sea views, Kecak fire dance at dusk, and serene temple paths.

  • Arrive 1 hour before sunset for the best light.
  • Carry a sarong; temple areas require modest clothing.

Ubud rice terraces

Green layers and quiet trails; mornings are cooler and less crowded.

  • Tegalalang at early hours for calm photos.
  • Small cafés with terrace views tucked in side lanes.

Waterfalls in the north

Twin falls and natural pools; shoes with grip help on wet paths.

  • Sekumpul or Tibumana for varied difficulty.
  • Keep cash for local entry points.

Simple 5-day itinerary

Day 1: Arrival + Uluwatu

Check-in, slow lunch, sunset at Uluwatu Temple, optional Kecak performance.

  • Evening: Cliffside views, easy strolls.
  • Food: Nasi Campur or fresh seafood near Jimbaran.

Day 2: Ubud terraces & temples

Sunrise at Tegalalang, brunch, then Tirta Empul or Goa Gajah.

  • Afternoon: Monkey Forest (watch belongings).
  • Evening: Ubud market lanes and cafés.

Day 3: Waterfalls + coffee

Head north for Sekumpul/Tibumana, then a local roastery tasting.

  • Pack: Light rain jacket, towel, sandals.
  • Return: Sunset stop at Campuhan Ridge walk.

Day 4: Beach day (Canggu)

Surf lesson or chill by the shore; cafés and street food after.

  • Try: Smoothie bowls, satay, fresh coconut.
  • Evening: Tanah Lot for a scenic sunset.

Day 5: Nusa Penida option

Early boat, coastal viewpoints, and natural pools; return by evening.

  • Comfortable shoes; roads can be bumpy.
  • Carry water and sunscreen for long stretches.

Budget snapshot (per person)

Stay

Guesthouses and mid-range stays are plentiful.

  • Typical: ₹2,500–₹6,000 per night
  • Tip: Check breakfast inclusion and location walkability.

Food

Local warungs are affordable and delicious.

  • Meals: ₹250–₹900
  • Cafés: ₹600–₹1,200

Transport

Mix of scooter rentals and day drivers.

  • Scooter/day: ₹800–₹1,200
  • Driver/day: ₹3,500–₹6,000

Prices vary by season and area; keep some buffer for activities and souvenirs.

Practical tips

Timing

  • Sunrise: Best for terraces and temples.
  • Midday: Hydrate, take shaded breaks.

Etiquette

  • Dress modestly at temples; carry a sarong.
  • Be mindful of offerings on sidewalks.

Pack light

  • Quick-dry clothes, sandals with grip.
  • Power adapter, sunscreen, hat.
